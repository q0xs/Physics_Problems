# Task 09.04 – Hubble's Law Distance

## Problem Statement

Using $v=H_0d$ with $H_0=70\text{ (km/s)/Mpc}$, find the distance where recession speed equals the speed of light.

## Theory

Hubble's law is linear in distance for this simplified calculation. Set $v=c$ and solve for $d$.

$$
d=\frac{c}{H_0}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
d=\frac{3.00\times10^5\text{ km/s}}{70\text{ (km/s)/Mpc}}=4.29\times10^3\text{ Mpc}
$$

$$
d=4.29\text{ Gpc}=14.0\text{ billion light-years}
$$

## Final Result

$$
d=4.29\times10^3\text{ Mpc}
$$

## Interpretation

This Hubble distance is a useful scale, but the observable universe requires a full cosmological model.
