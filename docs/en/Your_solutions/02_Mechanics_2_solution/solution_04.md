# Task 04 – Energy and Momentum in a Frictionless Descent Followed by a Sticky Collision

## Problem Statement

A block of mass $0.5\ \mathrm{kg}$ slides down a frictionless track from a height of $3.0\ \mathrm{m}$. At the bottom it collides and sticks to a second block of mass $1.5\ \mathrm{kg}$, which is initially at rest.

Determine the speed of the combined mass immediately after the collision.

## Theory

This problem contains two stages.

### Stage 1: Descent on a frictionless track

Since the track is frictionless, mechanical energy is conserved:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Thus the speed at the bottom is

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

### Stage 2: Perfectly inelastic collision

When two bodies stick together, momentum is conserved but kinetic energy is not conserved.

If $m_1$ moves with speed $v_1$ and $m_2$ is initially at rest, then after sticking together the common speed is

$$
v_f = \frac{m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Speed of the first block at the bottom

For the first block,

$$
m_1 = 0.5\ \mathrm{kg}
$$

and the initial height is

$$
h = 3.0\ \mathrm{m}
$$

Using energy conservation,

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

Substitute $g = 9.81\ \mathrm{m/s^2}$:

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.81 \cdot 3.0}
$$

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{58.86}
$$

$$
v_1 \approx 7.67\ \mathrm{m/s}
$$

### 2. Apply momentum conservation during the collision

The second block is initially at rest:

$$
m_2 = 1.5\ \mathrm{kg}, \qquad v_2 = 0
$$

Momentum conservation gives

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f
$$

Since $v_2 = 0$,

$$
m_1 v_1 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f
$$

Hence,

$$
v_f = \frac{m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v_f = \frac{0.5 \cdot 7.67}{0.5 + 1.5}
$$

$$
v_f = \frac{3.835}{2.0}
$$

$$
v_f \approx 1.92\ \mathrm{m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The speed of the combined mass immediately after the collision is

$$
v_f \approx 1.92\ \mathrm{m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The first block gains speed from gravitational potential energy during its descent. During the collision, momentum is conserved, but because the collision is perfectly inelastic, part of the kinetic energy is transformed into internal energy such as deformation, heat, and sound.
