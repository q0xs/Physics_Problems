# Task 11.06 – Quantum Numbers for n Equals 3

## Problem Statement

For $n=3$, list possible $l$ and $m_l$ values and count distinct electron states.

## Theory

For a given principal quantum number, $l$ ranges from $0$ to $n-1$, and $m_l$ ranges from $-l$ to $+l$.

$$
l=0,1,\ldots,n-1
$$

$$
m_l=-l,-l+1,\ldots,l
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

For $n=3$, $l=0,1,2$. The allowed $m_l$ values are: for $l=0$, $m_l=0$; for $l=1$, $m_l=-1,0,1$; for $l=2$, $m_l=-2,-1,0,1,2$.

$$
1+3+5=9\text{ orbital states}
$$

Including two spin states per orbital gives $18$ electron states.

## Final Result

There are $9$ orbital states, or $18$ electron states when spin is included.

## Interpretation

The number of orbital states in shell $n$ is $n^2$, and including spin gives $2n^2$.
