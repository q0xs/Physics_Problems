# Task 06.08 – AC Voltage Across a Resistor

## Problem Statement

The current in an AC circuit is $I(t)=2\sin(120\pi t)$. The circuit contains a single $50\,\Omega$ resistor. Find $V(t)$.

## Theory

For a pure resistor, voltage and current are in phase and related instantaneously by Ohm's law.

$$
V(t)=I(t)R
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
V(t)=\left[2\sin(120\pi t)\right](50)
$$

$$
V(t)=100\sin(120\pi t)\text{ V}
$$

## Final Result

$$
V(t)=100\sin(120\pi t)\text{ V}
$$

## Interpretation

The voltage has the same angular frequency and phase as the current because the circuit is purely resistive.
