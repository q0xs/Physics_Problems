# Task 10.03 – Muon Lifetime Dilation

## Problem Statement

A muon has rest-frame lifetime $2.20\,\mu\text{s}$ and travels at $0.990c$. Find its lifetime measured on Earth.

## Theory

The muon lifetime in its own frame is proper time. Earth measures the dilated time.

$$
\Delta t=\gamma\Delta\tau
$$

$$
\gamma=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\beta^2}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\gamma=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-0.990^2}}=7.09
$$

$$
\Delta t=(7.09)(2.20\,\mu\text{s})=15.6\,\mu\text{s}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\Delta t=15.6\,\mu\text{s}
$$

## Interpretation

Time dilation allows high-speed muons to survive longer in the laboratory frame.
