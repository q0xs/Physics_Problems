# Physics Problem: Trigonometry

## 1. Key Definitions and Formulas

A vector $\vec{A}$ has:
- magnitude $15$,
- angle $60^\circ$ with the horizontal axis.

For a vector of magnitude $A$ and angle $\theta$, its components are:

$$
A_x = A\cos\theta
$$

$$
A_y = A\sin\theta
$$

where:
- $A_x$ is the horizontal component,
- $A_y$ is the vertical component.

## 2. Solving the Problem

We need to calculate the horizontal and vertical components of the vector.

## 3. Step-by-Step Derivation

### Horizontal component

$$
A_x = 15\cos 60^\circ
$$

Since

$$
\cos 60^\circ = \frac{1}{2}
$$

we get:

$$
A_x = 15 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = 7.5
$$

### Vertical component

$$
A_y = 15\sin 60^\circ
$$

Since

$$
\sin 60^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

we get:

$$
A_y = 15 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

Approximate value:

$$
A_y \approx 12.99
$$

## 4. Final Answer

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 12.99
$$
