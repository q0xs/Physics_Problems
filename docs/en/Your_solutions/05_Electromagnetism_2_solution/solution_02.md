# Task 05.02 – Ampere's Law for Parallel Wires

## Problem Statement

Two long, parallel wires are $10 \text{ cm}$ apart and carry currents of $5 \text{ A}$ in opposite directions. Calculate the magnitude and direction of the magnetic field at a point midway between the wires.

## Theory

The magnetic field a distance $r$ from a long straight wire is found from Ampere's law. At the midpoint, each wire is $0.05\text{ m}$ away. For opposite currents, the two magnetic fields at the midpoint point in the same direction by the right-hand rule.

$$
B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

The field from one wire is

$$
B_1 = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(5)}{2\pi(0.05)}
$$

$$
B_1 = 2.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}
$$

The two contributions add at the midpoint:

$$
B_{\text{net}} = 2B_1 = 4.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}
$$

## Final Result

$$
B_{\text{net}} = 4.0 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}
$$

## Interpretation

The direction is perpendicular to the page and is determined by the right-hand rule. If the left wire carries current upward and the right wire downward, the field at the midpoint is into the page.
