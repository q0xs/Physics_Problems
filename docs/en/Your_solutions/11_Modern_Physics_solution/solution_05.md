# Task 11.05 – Heisenberg Uncertainty in Electron Velocity

## Problem Statement

Using $\Delta x\Delta p\ge\hbar/2$, find the minimum velocity uncertainty for an electron confined within $0.1\text{ nm}$.

## Theory

Momentum uncertainty is related to velocity uncertainty by $\Delta p=m\Delta v$ for nonrelativistic motion.

$$
\Delta x\Delta p\ge\frac{\hbar}{2}
$$

$$
\Delta v\ge\frac{\hbar}{2m\Delta x}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\Delta x=0.1\text{ nm}=1.0\times10^{-10}\text{ m}
$$

$$
\Delta v\ge\frac{1.055\times10^{-34}}{2(9.109\times10^{-31})(1.0\times10^{-10})}=5.79\times10^5\text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\Delta v_{min}=5.79\times10^5\text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

Confining an electron to atomic dimensions produces a very large unavoidable spread in velocity.
