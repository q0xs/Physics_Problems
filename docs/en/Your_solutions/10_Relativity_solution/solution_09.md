# Task 10.09 – Kinetic Time Dilation for an ISS Astronaut

## Problem Statement

An astronaut spends one year on the ISS moving at $7.66\text{ km/s}$. How much younger are they than a twin on Earth due to kinetic time dilation?

## Theory

At low speeds compared with $c$, the time difference is approximately $t v^2/(2c^2)$.

$$
\Delta t\approx t\frac{v^2}{2c^2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
t=3.156\times10^7\text{ s}
$$

$$
v=7.66\times10^3\text{ m/s}
$$

$$
\Delta t=(3.156\times10^7)\frac{(7.66\times10^3)^2}{2(3.00\times10^8)^2}=1.03\times10^{-2}\text{ s}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\Delta t\approx0.010\text{ s}
$$

## Interpretation

The kinetic effect is small for orbital speeds but measurable with precise clocks.
