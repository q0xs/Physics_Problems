# Task 06.13 – Transformer Turns Ratio

## Problem Statement

A transformer steps $120\text{ V}$ AC down to $9.0\text{ V}$ AC. If the primary has $400$ turns, find the required secondary turns.

## Theory

Transformer voltage ratio equals turns ratio.

$$
\frac{V_s}{V_p}=\frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
N_s=N_p\frac{V_s}{V_p}
$$

$$
N_s=400\frac{9.0}{120}=30
$$

## Final Result

$$
N_s=30\text{ turns}
$$

## Interpretation

A much smaller secondary turn count is required because the transformer reduces voltage.
