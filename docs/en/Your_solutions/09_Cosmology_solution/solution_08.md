# Task 09.08 – Milky Way Trivia Calculations

## Problem Statement

Find the Milky Way diameter-to-thickness ratio, stars per person if $200$ billion stars were divided equally among Earth's people, and time to count all stars at one per second.

## Theory

These are order-of-magnitude ratio and unit-conversion calculations.

$$
\text{ratio}=\frac{D}{h}
$$

$$
t=\frac{N}{\text{rate}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\frac{100000\text{ ly}}{1000\text{ ly}}=100
$$

$$
\frac{2.0\times10^{11}}{8.0\times10^9}=25
$$

$$
t=2.0\times10^{11}\text{ s}=6.34\times10^3\text{ yr}
$$

## Final Result

The ratio is about $100:1$, each person would receive about $25$ stars, and counting all stars would take about $6340$ years.

## Interpretation

The arithmetic emphasizes both the flatness and the enormous star count of the Milky Way.
