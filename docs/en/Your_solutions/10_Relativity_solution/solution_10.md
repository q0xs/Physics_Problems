# Task 10.10 – Gravitational Time Dilation at ISS Altitude

## Problem Statement

How much younger would the astronaut be than their twin due only to gravitational time dilation after one year at $400\text{ km}$ altitude?

## Theory

A clock higher in a gravitational field runs faster than one on Earth's surface by a small fractional amount.

$$
\frac{\Delta t}{t}\approx\frac{GM}{c^2}\left(\frac{1}{R_E}-\frac{1}{R_E+h}\right)
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\frac{\Delta t}{t}=4.10\times10^{-11}
$$

$$
\Delta t=(4.10\times10^{-11})(3.156\times10^7)=1.29\times10^{-3}\text{ s}
$$

## Final Result

Due only to gravity, the astronaut would be about $0.0013\text{ s}$ older, not younger.

## Interpretation

Gravity and velocity effects have opposite signs for the ISS; the kinetic effect is larger in magnitude.
