# Task 10.08 – Adding Velocities When One Object Moves at Light Speed

## Problem Statement

What happens to relative velocities if one object is moving at the speed of light?

## Theory

The relativistic velocity addition formula gives $c$ whenever a light-speed signal is transformed between inertial frames.

$$
u=\frac{u^\prime+v}{1+u^\prime v/c^2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Set $u^\prime=c$:

$$
u=\frac{c+v}{1+v/c}=c
$$

## Final Result

$$
u=c
$$

## Interpretation

No inertial observer measures a light signal moving faster or slower than $c$ in vacuum.
