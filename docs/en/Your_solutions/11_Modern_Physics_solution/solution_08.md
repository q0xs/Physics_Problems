# Task 11.08 – Photon from a Quantum Well Transition

## Problem Statement

An electron in a one-dimensional infinite well of width $0.5\text{ nm}$ transitions from $n=4$ to $n=2$. Find the emitted photon wavelength.

## Theory

The infinite-well energy levels scale as $n^2$. The photon energy equals the level difference.

$$
E_n=\frac{n^2h^2}{8mL^2}
$$

$$
\Delta E=E_4-E_2=\frac{(16-4)h^2}{8mL^2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\Delta E=2.89\times10^{-18}\text{ J}=18.05\text{ eV}
$$

$$
\lambda=\frac{hc}{\Delta E}=6.87\times10^{-8}\text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\lambda=68.7\text{ nm}
$$

## Interpretation

The photon is ultraviolet because the well is very narrow, making energy spacings large.
