# Task 03 – Superposition Principle

## Problem Statement

Two waves are given:

$$
y_1 = A \sin(kx - \omega t), \quad y_2 = A \sin(kx + \omega t)
$$

Find the resulting standing wave and identify nodes.

## Theory

Using the identity:

$$
\sin a + \sin b = 2 \sin\left(\frac{a+b}{2}\right) \cos\left(\frac{a-b}{2}\right)
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
y = y_1 + y_2
$$

$$
y = A \sin(kx - \omega t) + A \sin(kx + \omega t)
$$

Apply identity:

$$
y = 2A \sin(kx) \cos(\omega t)
$$

### Nodes

Nodes occur when displacement is always zero:

$$
\sin(kx) = 0
$$

$$
kx = n\pi
$$

$$
x = \frac{n\pi}{k} = n\frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

## Final Result

Standing wave:

$$
y = 2A \sin(kx) \cos(\omega t)
$$

Nodes:

$$
x = n\frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

## Interpretation

Nodes are fixed points where destructive interference occurs at all times.
