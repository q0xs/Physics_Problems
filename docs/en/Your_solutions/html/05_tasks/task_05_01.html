Task 05.01 – Gauss's Law for a Point Charge

Electric flux through a spherical Gaussian surface

Interactive Visualization

Electric flux:
Φ = 2.26 × 10¹¹ N·m²/C

Problem

A point charge of $+2\\,\\mathrm{C}$ is located at the origin. Calculate the electric flux through a spherical surface of radius $1\\,\\mathrm{m}$ centered at the origin.

Theory

Gauss's Law states that the total electric flux through a closed surface depends only on the charge enclosed by that surface:

$$\\Phi_E = \\oint_S \\vec{E}\\cdot d\\vec{A} = \\frac{q_{enc}}{\\varepsilon_0}$$

Solution

Given:
$q_{enc} = 2\\,\\mathrm{C}$
$\\varepsilon_0 = 8.854 \\times 10^{-12}\\,\\mathrm{C^2/(N\\,m^2)}$

$$\\Phi_E = \\frac{2}{8.854 \\times 10^{-12}}$$

$$\\Phi_E = 2.26 \\times 10^{11}\\,\\mathrm{N\\,m^2/C}$$

Interpretation

The flux is positive because the electric field lines leave the closed surface. The radius does not affect the final flux because the electric field decreases as $1/r^2$, while the spherical area increases as $r^2$.
