Potential Energy & Height

Mechanical energy \(E\) at the peak of a bounce is purely gravitational potential energy:

\[E = mgh \implies h \propto E\]

Because \(m\) and \(g\) are constant, any fractional loss in energy results in the same fractional loss in height.

Geometric Sequences

If the ball retains a fraction \(r\) of its energy after each bounce, the heights form a geometric progression:

\[h_n = h_0 \cdot r^n\]

In this specific task:

\[h_0 = 2.0, \quad r = 1 - 0.30 = 0.70\]
\[h_2 = 2.0 \cdot (0.70)^2 = 0.98\ \mathrm{m}\]
