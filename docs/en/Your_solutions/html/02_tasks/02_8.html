The Restoring Force

Hooke's Law describes a force that is directly proportional to displacement but in the opposite direction:

\[F(x) = -kx\]

Work & Potential Energy

The work done to displace the mass is stored as potential energy \(U\):

\[U(x) = -\int_0^x F(x')dx' = \int_0^x kx' dx' = \frac{1}{2}kx^2\]

This quadratic relationship creates a parabolic potential well, which is the hallmark of any stable equilibrium point in nature.
