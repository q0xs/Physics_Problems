# Task 07.04 – Relative Uncertainty of a Speedometer

## Problem Statement

A car speedometer has $5\%$ uncertainty. If it reads $60\text{ km/h}$, find the possible range of actual speed.

## Theory

Percentage uncertainty is converted to absolute uncertainty by multiplying by the measured value.

$$
\Delta v = \left(\frac{p}{100}\right)v
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\Delta v=0.05(60)=3\text{ km/h}
$$

$$
v_{\min}=60-3=57\text{ km/h}
$$

$$
v_{\max}=60+3=63\text{ km/h}
$$

## Final Result

The actual speed range is $57\text{ km/h}$ to $63\text{ km/h}$.

## Interpretation

The instrument reading gives a central estimate, not an exact value.
