# Task 09 – Momentum Comparison

## Problem Statement

Which has greater momentum:

1. a $2$-gram fly flying at $10 \text{ m/s}$,
2. a $60$-gram tennis ball moving at $1 \text{ m/s}$?

## Theory

Linear momentum is defined by

$$
p = mv
$$

where:

- $m$ is the mass,
- $v$ is the speed.

To compare momenta, the masses must be converted to SI units.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Momentum of the fly

The fly has mass

$$
m_f = 2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}
$$

and speed

$$
v_f = 10 \text{ m/s}
$$

Thus its momentum is

$$
p_f = m_f v_f = 0.002 \cdot 10
$$

$$
p_f = 0.020 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

### 2. Momentum of the tennis ball

The tennis ball has mass

$$
m_t = 60 \text{ g} = 0.060 \text{ kg}
$$

and speed

$$
v_t = 1 \text{ m/s}
$$

Thus its momentum is

$$
p_t = m_t v_t = 0.060 \cdot 1
$$

$$
p_t = 0.060 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

### 3. Comparison

Compare the two values:

$$
p_f = 0.020 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

$$
p_t = 0.060 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

Since

$$
0.060 > 0.020
$$

the tennis ball has the greater momentum.

## Final Result

The tennis ball has the greater momentum.

Fly:

$$
p_f = 0.020 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

Tennis ball:

$$
p_t = 0.060 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

Although the fly moves much faster, its mass is very small. Since momentum depends on both mass and speed, the larger mass of the tennis ball more than compensates for its smaller speed. In fact, the tennis ball's momentum is three times the fly's momentum.
