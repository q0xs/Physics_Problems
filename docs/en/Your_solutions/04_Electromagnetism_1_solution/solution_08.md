# Task 08 – Magnitude of the Lorentz Force

## Problem Statement

A charged particle with

$$
q = 2 \times 10^{-19}\,\mathrm{C}
$$

and

$$
m = 4 \times 10^{-27}\,\mathrm{kg}
$$

enters a magnetic field of

$$
B = 0.5\,\mathrm{T}
$$

with speed

$$
v = 1.0 \times 10^6\,\mathrm{m/s}
$$

perpendicular to the field. Determine the magnitude of the Lorentz force.

## Theory

The magnetic part of the Lorentz force is

$$
F = qvB\sin\theta
$$

where $\theta$ is the angle between the velocity vector and the magnetic field vector.

Because the particle enters perpendicular to the field,

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

and therefore

$$
\sin\theta = 1
$$

So the magnitude reduces to

$$
F = qvB
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute the values directly:

$$
F = (2 \times 10^{-19})(1.0 \times 10^6)(0.5)
$$

Combine the numerical factors:

$$
2 \cdot 1.0 \cdot 0.5 = 1.0
$$

Combine the power of ten:

$$
10^{-19} \cdot 10^6 = 10^{-13}
$$

Thus,

$$
F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

## Final Result

The magnitude of the Lorentz force is

$$
F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

## Interpretation

Since the velocity is perpendicular to the field, the magnetic force has its maximum value for the given speed and field strength. The force changes only the direction of motion, not the speed, so the particle would undergo uniform circular motion in a uniform magnetic field.
