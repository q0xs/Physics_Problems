# Task 11.04 – Bohr Radius and Angular Momentum for n Equals 2

## Problem Statement

Using the Bohr model, determine orbital radius and angular momentum for hydrogen at $n=2$.

## Theory

In the Bohr model, radius scales as $n^2$ and angular momentum is quantized in units of $\hbar$.

$$
r_n=n^2a_0
$$

$$
L_n=n\hbar
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
r_2=2^2a_0=4a_0
$$

$$
a_0=5.29\times10^{-11}\text{ m}
$$

$$
r_2=2.12\times10^{-10}\text{ m}
$$

$$
L_2=2\hbar
$$

## Final Result

$$
r_2=4a_0=2.12\times10^{-10}\text{ m}
$$

$$
L_2=2\hbar
$$

## Interpretation

The $n=2$ orbit is four times the Bohr radius and has twice the ground-state angular momentum.
