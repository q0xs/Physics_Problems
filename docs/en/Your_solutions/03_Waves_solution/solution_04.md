# Task 04 – Phase Difference

## Problem Statement

Determine the phase difference between two points on a wave that are separated by a distance of $\lambda/3$.

---

## Physical Background

A sinusoidal wave repeats itself every wavelength $\lambda$.  
This means that a full wavelength corresponds to a phase change of $2\pi$ radians.

Therefore, any fraction of the wavelength produces a proportional phase difference.

---

## Theory

The phase difference between two points separated by a distance $\Delta x$ is given by:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \Delta x
$$

This equation shows that phase difference is directly proportional to spatial separation.

---

## Normalized Form

If the distance is expressed as a fraction of the wavelength:

$$
\Delta x = r\lambda
$$

then the phase difference becomes:

$$
\Delta \phi = 2\pi r
$$

This form simplifies calculations and clearly shows the linear relationship.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

Given:

$$
\Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

Substitute into the phase formula:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

Cancel $\lambda$:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3} \, \text{rad}
$$

---

## Conversion to Degrees

Since:

$$
2\pi \, \text{rad} = 360^\circ
$$

we obtain:

$$
\frac{2\pi}{3} = 120^\circ
$$

---

## Interpretation

A separation of $\lambda/3$ corresponds to one-third of a full wavelength.

Since one full wavelength represents a complete phase cycle of $2\pi$ radians, one-third of a wavelength produces a phase shift of:

- $\frac{2\pi}{3}$ radians  
- $120^\circ$

This confirms that phase difference increases linearly with distance along the wave.

---

## Key Insight

- $\lambda \rightarrow 2\pi$ radians  
- $\frac{\lambda}{3} \rightarrow \frac{2\pi}{3}$ radians  

👉 Spatial fraction = same phase fraction
