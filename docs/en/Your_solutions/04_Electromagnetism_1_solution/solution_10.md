# Task 10 – Magnetic Force on a Current-Carrying Wire

## Problem Statement

A straight wire of length

$$
L = 2.0\,\mathrm{m}
$$

carries a current

$$
I = 10\,\mathrm{A}
$$

and is placed in a uniform magnetic field

$$
B = 0.5\,\mathrm{T}
$$

Calculate the magnetic force on the wire when the angle between the wire and the magnetic field is:

a) $90^\circ$

b) $45^\circ$

c) $0^\circ$

## Theory

The magnetic force on a straight current-carrying wire in a uniform magnetic field is

$$
F = ILB \sin\theta
$$

where $\theta$ is the angle between the current direction and the magnetic field.

The force is largest when the wire is perpendicular to the field and vanishes when the wire is parallel to the field.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Compute the common factor

First calculate

$$
ILB = (10)(2.0)(0.5)
$$

$$
ILB = 10
$$

Thus the force formula becomes

$$
F = 10 \sin\theta
$$

with force measured in newtons.

### Case a: $\theta = 90^\circ$

Since

$$
\sin 90^\circ = 1
$$

the force is

$$
F = 10 \cdot 1
$$

$$
F = 10\,\mathrm{N}
$$

### Case b: $\theta = 45^\circ$

Since

$$
\sin 45^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707
$$

the force becomes

$$
F = 10 \cdot 0.707
$$

$$
F \approx 7.07\,\mathrm{N}
$$

### Case c: $\theta = 0^\circ$

Since

$$
\sin 0^\circ = 0
$$

the force is

$$
F = 10 \cdot 0
$$

$$
F = 0\,\mathrm{N}
$$

## Final Result

The magnetic force on the wire is:

### a) For $90^\circ$

$$
F = 10\,\mathrm{N}
$$

### b) For $45^\circ$

$$
F \approx 7.07\,\mathrm{N}
$$

### c) For $0^\circ$

$$
F = 0\,\mathrm{N}
$$

## Interpretation

Only the component of the wire perpendicular to the magnetic field contributes to the magnetic force. The sine factor encodes this geometry directly. A wire parallel to the field experiences no magnetic force, while a perpendicular wire experiences the maximum force.
