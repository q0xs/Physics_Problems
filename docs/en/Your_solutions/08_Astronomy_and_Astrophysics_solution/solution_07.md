# Task 08.07 – Radius of a Dyson Sphere from Mercury's Mass

## Problem Statement

If Mercury's mass $3.3\times10^{23}\text{ kg}$ is used to build a spherical shell with surface density $10\text{ kg/m}^2$, find its radius.

## Theory

The mass of a spherical shell equals surface density multiplied by area.

$$
M=4\pi R^2\sigma
$$

$$
R=\sqrt{\frac{M}{4\pi\sigma}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
R=\sqrt{\frac{3.3\times10^{23}}{4\pi(10)}}
$$

$$
R=5.12\times10^{10}\text{ m}=5.12\times10^7\text{ km}
$$

## Final Result

$$
R=5.12\times10^{10}\text{ m}
$$

## Interpretation

This radius is about $0.34$ astronomical units, showing the extreme scale of such a structure.
