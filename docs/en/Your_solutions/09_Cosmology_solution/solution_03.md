# Task 09.03 – Ordering Cosmic Sizes

## Problem Statement

Order Earth, Milky Way, Solar System, observable universe, Sun, Andromeda, human, Moon, Pluto, Jupiter, Ceres, and Local Group from smallest to largest.

## Theory

The comparison uses characteristic diameters or extents. Planets and dwarf planets are ordered by physical diameter, while galaxies and groups are ordered by spatial extent.

$$
\text{size scale: human} < \text{planetary bodies} < \text{stellar systems} < \text{galaxies} < \text{universe}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Using approximate sizes gives the ordering from meters to billions of light-years.

## Final Result

Human, Ceres, Pluto, Moon, Earth, Jupiter, Sun, Solar System, Milky Way galaxy, Andromeda galaxy, Local Group, observable universe.

## Interpretation

The jumps between categories are enormous; the Solar System is already tiny compared with galactic scales.
