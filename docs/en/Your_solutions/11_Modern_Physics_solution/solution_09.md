# Task 11.09 – Electron-Positron Pair Annihilation

## Problem Statement

An electron and positron annihilate into two photons of equal energy. Find each photon energy and wavelength.

## Theory

At rest, the total rest energy is shared equally between two photons, so each photon gets one particle rest energy.

$$
E_\gamma=0.511\text{ MeV}
$$

$$
\lambda=\frac{hc}{E}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
E_\gamma=0.511\text{ MeV}=8.19\times10^{-14}\text{ J}
$$

$$
\lambda=\frac{(6.626\times10^{-34})(3.00\times10^8)}{8.19\times10^{-14}}=2.43\times10^{-12}\text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

Each photon has energy $0.511\text{ MeV}$ and wavelength $2.43\text{ pm}$.

## Interpretation

The two photons move in opposite directions to conserve momentum.
