# Task 07.02 – Uncertainty in the Area of a Rectangle

## Problem Statement

The length and width of a plate are $L=(15.3\pm0.1)\text{ cm}$ and $W=(8.4\pm0.1)\text{ cm}$. Calculate area and uncertainty.

## Theory

For independent uncertainties in a product, relative uncertainties combine in quadrature.

$$
A=LW
$$

$$
\frac{\Delta A}{A}=\sqrt{\left(\frac{\Delta L}{L}\right)^2+\left(\frac{\Delta W}{W}\right)^2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
A=(15.3)(8.4)=128.5\text{ cm}^2
$$

$$
\frac{\Delta A}{A}=\sqrt{(0.1/15.3)^2+(0.1/8.4)^2}=0.0136
$$

$$
\Delta A=(128.5)(0.0136)=1.75\text{ cm}^2
$$

## Final Result

$$
A=(128.5\pm1.8)\text{ cm}^2
$$

## Interpretation

The width contributes more relative uncertainty than the length because the same absolute uncertainty is applied to a smaller measurement.
