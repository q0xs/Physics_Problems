# Task 02 – Magnetic Field Between Two Parallel Wires

## Problem Statement

Two long, parallel wires are $10 \text{ cm}$ apart and carry currents of $5 \text{ A}$ in opposite directions. Calculate the magnitude and direction of the magnetic field at a point midway between the wires.

## Theory

The magnetic field produced by a long straight wire at a distance $r$ is

$$
B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2 \pi r}
$$

where

- $B$ is the magnetic field magnitude,
- $\mu_0 = 4 \pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T m/A}$ is the permeability of free space,
- $I$ is the current,
- $r$ is the perpendicular distance from the wire.

The total magnetic field at a point is obtained by vector addition of the individual fields from both wires.

The direction is determined by the right-hand rule. Because the currents are in opposite directions, the fields at the midpoint point in the same direction, so they add.

## Step-by-Step Solution

The wires are separated by

$$
d = 10 \text{ cm} = 0.10 \text{ m}
$$

The midpoint is at a distance

$$
r = \frac{d}{2} = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

Each wire carries current

$$
I = 5 \text{ A}
$$

### Magnetic field due to one wire

Using

$$
B_1 = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2 \pi r}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
B_1 = \frac{4 \pi \times 10^{-7} \times 5}{2 \pi \times 0.05}
$$

Simplify:

$$
B_1 = \frac{20 \pi \times 10^{-7}}{0.1 \pi}
$$

$$
B_1 = 2 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}
$$

### Total magnetic field

Since the magnetic fields at the midpoint are in the same direction, the total field is

$$
B_{\text{total}} = B_1 + B_2 = 2B_1
$$

$$
B_{\text{total}} = 2 \times 2 \times 10^{-5}
$$

$$
B_{\text{total}} = 4.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}
$$

### Direction

Apply the right-hand rule to each wire. At the midpoint, the magnetic field from one wire points into the page, and the field from the other wire also points into the page, provided one wire carries current upward and the other downward in the usual side-by-side arrangement.

Thus the net magnetic field is into the page.

## Final Result

$$
B_{\text{total}} = 4.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}
$$

Direction: into the page.

## Interpretation

Because the currents flow in opposite directions, the magnetic fields between the wires reinforce one another. This produces a stronger field at the midpoint than would occur if the currents had the same direction. The result shows the importance of vector addition and field direction in magnetostatics.
