Task 05.10 – Speed of Light in Diamond

How refractive index changes the speed of light inside a material

Interactive Visualization

Speed of light in the medium:
v = 1.24 × 10⁸ m/s

Problem

Find the speed of light in diamond, which has refractive index $n = 2.42$.

Theory

The refractive index compares the speed of light in vacuum with the speed of light inside a medium.

$$n = \frac{c}{v}$$

$$v = \frac{c}{n}$$

Step-by-Step Solution

Given:
$c = 3.00 \times 10^8\,\mathrm{m/s}$
$n = 2.42$

$$v = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{2.42}$$

$$v = 1.24 \times 10^8\,\mathrm{m/s}$$

Interpretation

Light travels more slowly in diamond than in vacuum because diamond has a high refractive index. The higher the refractive index, the slower light travels inside the material.
