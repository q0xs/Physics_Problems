# Task 06.09 – Current from a Charge Function

## Problem Statement

Charge flowing through a wire is $Q(t)=5t^2+5$. Find the current at $t=3\text{ s}$.

## Theory

Current is the time derivative of charge.

$$
I(t)=\frac{dQ}{dt}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
I(t)=\frac{d}{dt}(5t^2+5)=10t
$$

$$
I(3)=10(3)=30\text{ A}
$$

## Final Result

$$
I(3)=30\text{ A}
$$

## Interpretation

The charge flow rate increases linearly with time because the charge function is quadratic.
