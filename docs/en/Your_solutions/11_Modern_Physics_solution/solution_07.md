# Task 11.07 – Hydrogen Emission from n Equals 4 to n Equals 2

## Problem Statement

Find the photon energy and wavelength for a hydrogen transition from $n=4$ to $n=2$, and identify the color.

## Theory

Hydrogen energy levels are $E_n=-13.6\text{ eV}/n^2$. The photon energy is the difference between final and initial level energies.

$$
E_n=-\frac{13.6\text{ eV}}{n^2}
$$

$$
E_\gamma=E_i-E_f
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
E_4=-13.6/16=-0.85\text{ eV}
$$

$$
E_2=-13.6/4=-3.40\text{ eV}
$$

$$
E_\gamma=(-0.85)-(-3.40)=2.55\text{ eV}
$$

$$
\lambda=\frac{1240\text{ eV nm}}{2.55\text{ eV}}=486\text{ nm}
$$

## Final Result

The photon has energy $2.55\text{ eV}$ and wavelength $486\text{ nm}$, which is blue-green visible light.

## Interpretation

This is the Balmer $H_\beta$ line.
