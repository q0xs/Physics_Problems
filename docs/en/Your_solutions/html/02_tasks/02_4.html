Physics of the Descent

In a frictionless environment, the potential energy at height \(h\) is converted entirely into kinetic energy at the bottom (\(h=0\)).

\[m_1gh = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2 \implies v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}\]

Momentum Conservation

During the collision, no external horizontal forces act on the system. Therefore, the total momentum remains constant, even though kinetic energy is lost to heat and deformation (Inelastic Collision).

\[p_{initial} = p_{final}\]
\[m_1v_1 + m_2(0) = (m_1 + m_2)v_f\]
\[v_f = \frac{m_1v_1}{m_1 + m_2}\]
