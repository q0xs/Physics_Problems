# Task 09.11 – Peak Wavelength of the CMB

## Problem Statement

The CMB temperature is now $2.7\text{ K}$ and was about $3000\text{ K}$ at recombination. Use Wien's law to find peak wavelengths.

## Theory

Wien's displacement law states that the peak wavelength is inversely proportional to temperature.

$$
\lambda_{peak}=\frac{b}{T}
$$

$$
b=2.898\times10^{-3}\text{ m K}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\lambda_{rec}=\frac{2.898\times10^{-3}}{3000}=9.66\times10^{-7}\text{ m}
$$

$$
\lambda_{now}=\frac{2.898\times10^{-3}}{2.7}=1.07\times10^{-3}\text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

At recombination the peak was $9.66\times10^{-7}\text{ m}$; today it is $1.07\text{ mm}$.

## Interpretation

Cosmic expansion stretches photon wavelengths, shifting the CMB from near-infrared/visible scale to microwaves.
