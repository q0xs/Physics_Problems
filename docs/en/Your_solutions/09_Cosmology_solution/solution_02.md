# Task 09.02 – Ordering Cosmic Temperatures

## Problem Statement

Order listed cosmic temperatures from hottest to coldest: Sun core, Sun surface, CMB, interstellar space, nucleosynthesis era, human body, recombination era, ten-solar-mass black hole, ionization temperature of hydrogen.

## Theory

Temperature ordering compares characteristic thermal scales. The hydrogen ionization temperature corresponds to $13.6\text{ eV}/k_B$.

$$
T=\frac{E}{k_B}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Approximate scales are: nucleosynthesis era $\sim10^9\text{ K}$, Sun core $\sim1.5\times10^7\text{ K}$, hydrogen ionization $\sim1.6\times10^5\text{ K}$, Sun surface $\sim5800\text{ K}$, recombination $\sim3000\text{ K}$, human body $\sim310\text{ K}$, interstellar gas $\sim10\text{ K}$, CMB $2.7\text{ K}$, and a ten-solar-mass black hole $\sim10^{-8}\text{ K}$.

## Final Result

Hottest to coldest: nucleosynthesis era, Sun core, hydrogen ionization temperature, Sun surface, recombination era, human body, interstellar space, CMB, ten-solar-mass black hole.

## Interpretation

The universe cools as it expands, while compact objects such as stellar cores and black holes have very different thermal scales.
