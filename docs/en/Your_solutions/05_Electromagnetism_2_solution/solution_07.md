# Task 07 – Wavelength, Color, and Frequency of Light

## Problem Statement

The human eye is most sensitive to light with a wavelength of about $550 \text{ nm}$. What color does this correspond to in the visible spectrum? What is the frequency of this light?

## Theory

Visible light occupies only a small part of the electromagnetic spectrum. Different wavelengths correspond to different perceived colors.

The frequency of light is related to wavelength by

$$
f = \frac{c}{\lambda}
$$

where

- $f$ is the frequency,
- $c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$ is the speed of light in vacuum,
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Color identification

The wavelength is

$$
\lambda = 550 \text{ nm}
$$

Convert to meters:

$$
\lambda = 550 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}
$$

$$
\lambda = 5.50 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}
$$

A wavelength of about $550 \text{ nm}$ corresponds to green light, near the yellow-green region of the visible spectrum.

### 2. Frequency calculation

Use

$$
f = \frac{c}{\lambda}
$$

Substitute:

$$
f = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8}{5.50 \times 10^{-7}}
$$

Rewrite the denominator calculation carefully:

$$
f = 3.0 \times 10^8 \times \frac{1}{5.50 \times 10^{-7}}
$$

$$
f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}
$$

## Final Result

Color:

$$
\text{green light}
$$

Frequency:

$$
f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}
$$

## Interpretation

A wavelength of $550 \text{ nm}$ lies near the region of maximum human visual sensitivity, which is why green-yellow light is perceived very efficiently by the eye. The corresponding frequency is in the hundreds of terahertz range, which is typical for visible light.
