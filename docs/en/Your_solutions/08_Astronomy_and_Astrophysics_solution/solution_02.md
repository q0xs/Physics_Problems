# Task 08.02 – Orbital Speed of the ISS and Earth

## Problem Statement

Calculate the orbital speed of the ISS at altitude $400\text{ km}$ and compare it with Earth's orbital speed around the Sun.

## Theory

Circular orbital speed follows from setting gravity equal to centripetal force.

$$
v=\sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}
$$

$$
v=\frac{2\pi R}{T}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
r_{ISS}=6378\text{ km}+400\text{ km}=6.778\times10^6\text{ m}
$$

$$
v_{ISS}=\sqrt{\frac{(6.674\times10^{-11})(5.97\times10^{24})}{6.778\times10^6}}=7.67\text{ km/s}
$$

$$
v_{Earth}=\frac{2\pi(1.50\times10^8\text{ km})}{365.25\times24\times3600\text{ s}}=29.9\text{ km/s}
$$

## Final Result

The ISS speed is $7.67\text{ km/s}$ and Earth's orbital speed is $29.9\text{ km/s}$, so Earth around the Sun is faster.

## Interpretation

The ISS is close to Earth and orbits quickly, but Earth's much larger solar orbit still corresponds to a higher linear speed.
