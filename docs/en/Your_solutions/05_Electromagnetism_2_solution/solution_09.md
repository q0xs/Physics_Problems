# Task 05.09 – Snell's Law Refraction

## Problem Statement

A light ray travels from air $n=1.00$ into glass $n=1.50$. If the angle of incidence is $30^\circ$, find the angle of refraction.

## Theory

Snell's law relates the incident and refracted angles measured from the normal. When light enters a higher-index medium, it bends toward the normal.

$$
n_1\sin\theta_1 = n_2\sin\theta_2
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\sin\theta_2 = \frac{n_1}{n_2}\sin\theta_1
$$

$$
\sin\theta_2 = \frac{1.00}{1.50}\sin30^\circ = \frac{1}{3}
$$

$$
\theta_2 = \sin^{-1}(0.333) = 19.5^\circ
$$

## Final Result

$$
\theta_2 = 19.5^\circ
$$

## Interpretation

The refracted angle is smaller than the incident angle because glass has a larger refractive index than air.
