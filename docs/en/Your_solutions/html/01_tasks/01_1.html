Mathematical Background

Equations of Motion

Horizontal position: $x(t) = v_0 \cos(\theta) t$

Vertical position: $y(t) = v_0 \sin(\theta) t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$

Since air resistance is neglected ($F_{drag}=0$), horizontal velocity remains constant.

Key Derived Values

  • $T = \frac{2v_0 \sin\theta}{g}$
  • $H_{max} = \frac{(v_0 \sin\theta)^2}{2g}$
  • $R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$
