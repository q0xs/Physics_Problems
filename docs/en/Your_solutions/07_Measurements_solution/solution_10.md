# Task 07.10 – Microwave Measurement of Light Speed

## Problem Statement

Use a microwave oven and melted spots separated by distance $d$ to estimate the speed of light, using $f=2.45\text{ GHz}$.

## Theory

Adjacent melted spots are separated by half a wavelength, so $\lambda=2d$. The wave speed is $c=f\lambda$.

$$
\lambda=2d
$$

$$
c=f\lambda=2fd
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

For a representative measured spot spacing $d=6.1\text{ cm}=0.061\text{ m}$,

$$
\lambda=2(0.061)=0.122\text{ m}
$$

$$
c=(2.45\times10^9)(0.122)=2.99\times10^8\text{ m/s}
$$

$$
\text{percentage error}=\frac{|2.99-3.00|}{3.00}\times100\%=0.3\%
$$

## Final Result

$$
c\approx2.99\times10^8\text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The method is approximate because the oven field pattern is not perfectly uniform and spot spacing must be measured carefully.
