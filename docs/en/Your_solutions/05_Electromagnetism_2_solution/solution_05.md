# Task 05.05 – Energy Stored in a Parallel-Plate Capacitor

## Problem Statement

A parallel-plate capacitor has plate area $S=0.02\,\mathrm{m^2}$, separation $d=5\,\mathrm{mm}$, and voltage $U=500\,\mathrm{V}$. Calculate the capacitance, stored energy, electric field, and attractive force between the plates.

## Theory

For a parallel-plate capacitor in air, capacitance depends on geometry. Stored energy depends on capacitance and voltage. The electric field between plates is approximately uniform, and the attractive force follows from electric pressure.

$$
C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 S}{d}
$$

$$
W = \frac{1}{2}CU^2
$$

$$
E = \frac{U}{d}
$$

$$
F = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2 S
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Convert the separation: $d=5\text{ mm}=5.0\times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$. Then

$$
C = \frac{(8.854\times10^{-12})(0.02)}{5.0\times10^{-3}} = 3.54\times10^{-11}\text{ F}
$$

$$
W = \frac{1}{2}(3.54\times10^{-11})(500)^2 = 4.43\times10^{-6}\text{ J}
$$

$$
E = \frac{500}{5.0\times10^{-3}} = 1.00\times10^5\text{ V/m}
$$

$$
F = \frac{1}{2}(8.854\times10^{-12})(1.00\times10^5)^2(0.02) = 8.85\times10^{-4}\text{ N}
$$

## Final Result

$$
C = 3.54\times10^{-11}\text{ F}
$$

$$
W = 4.43\times10^{-6}\text{ J}
$$

$$
E = 1.00\times10^5\text{ V/m}
$$

$$
F = 8.85\times10^{-4}\text{ N}
$$

## Interpretation

The small capacitance reflects the millimeter plate separation and modest area. The force is attractive because opposite charges are stored on facing plates.
