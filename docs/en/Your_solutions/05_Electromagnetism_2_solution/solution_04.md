# Task 05.04 – Magnetic Torque on a Current Loop

## Problem Statement

A rectangular loop of wire with dimensions $10 \text{ cm}$ by $5 \text{ cm}$ carries a current of $2 \text{ A}$. A uniform magnetic field of $0.3 \text{ T}$ is applied parallel to the plane of the loop. Find the magnitude of the magnetic torque.

## Theory

The torque on a current loop is the cross product of its magnetic dipole moment and the magnetic field. The magnetic moment is perpendicular to the plane of the loop. If the field is parallel to the plane, the angle between $\vec{\mu}$ and $\vec{B}$ is $90^\circ$.

$$
\tau = IAB\sin\theta
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

The area of the loop is

$$
A = (0.10)(0.05) = 5.0 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}^2
$$

With $\theta=90^\circ$,

$$
\tau = (2)(5.0 \times 10^{-3})(0.3)(1)
$$

$$
\tau = 3.0 \times 10^{-3}\text{ N m}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\tau = 3.0 \times 10^{-3}\text{ N m}
$$

## Interpretation

The loop tends to rotate until its magnetic moment aligns with the field, which means the plane of the loop becomes perpendicular to the magnetic field.
