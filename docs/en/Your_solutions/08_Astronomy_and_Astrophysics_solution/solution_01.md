# Task 08.01 – Rotational Velocity at Earth's Equator

## Problem Statement

Calculate the linear speed in $\text{km/s}$ of a point on Earth's equator due to rotation. Use $R=6378\text{ km}$.

## Theory

A point on the equator moves one full circumference in one rotation period.

$$
v=\frac{2\pi R}{T}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
T=24\text{ h}=86400\text{ s}
$$

$$
v=\frac{2\pi(6378)}{86400}=0.464\text{ km/s}
$$

## Final Result

$$
v=0.464\text{ km/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The speed is largest at the equator because the distance from the rotation axis is largest.
