# Task 10.01 – Relativistic Length Contraction

## Problem Statement

At what speed must a $1\text{ m}$ ruler travel for its observed length to be $0.5\text{ m}$?

## Theory

Length contraction is controlled by the Lorentz factor. The contracted length is measured only along the direction of motion.

$$
L=\frac{L_0}{\gamma}
$$

$$
\gamma=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\frac{L}{L_0}=0.5=\sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}}
$$

$$
0.25=1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}
$$

$$
v=0.866c
$$

## Final Result

$$
v=0.866c
$$

## Interpretation

A factor-of-two contraction requires a speed very close to the speed of light.
