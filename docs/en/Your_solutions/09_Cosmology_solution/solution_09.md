# Task 09.09 – Distance Increase from Cosmic Expansion

## Problem Statement

If two galaxies are $100$ million light-years apart and $H_0=70\text{ km/s/Mpc}$, estimate the distance increase after $1$ billion years assuming constant expansion rate.

## Theory

Convert the separation to megaparsecs, use Hubble's law for recession speed, then multiply by time.

$$
v=H_0d
$$

$$
\Delta d=vt
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
d=\frac{100\text{ million ly}}{3.26\text{ million ly/Mpc}}=30.7\text{ Mpc}
$$

$$
v=70(30.7)=2147\text{ km/s}
$$

$$
\Delta d=\frac{2147}{3.00\times10^5}(10^9)\text{ ly}=7.16\times10^6\text{ ly}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\Delta d\approx7.2\text{ million light-years}
$$

## Interpretation

This is a simplified constant-rate estimate; real expansion changes with cosmic time.
