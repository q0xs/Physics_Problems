# Task 06.10 – Average Current in a Lightning Bolt

## Problem Statement

A lightning bolt transfers $30\text{ C}$ to the ground in $2\text{ ms}$. Find the average current.

## Theory

Average current is charge transferred per unit time.

$$
I_{\text{avg}}=\frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\Delta t=2\text{ ms}=2.0\times10^{-3}\text{ s}
$$

$$
I_{\text{avg}}=\frac{30}{2.0\times10^{-3}}=1.5\times10^4\text{ A}
$$

## Final Result

$$
I_{\text{avg}}=1.5\times10^4\text{ A}
$$

## Interpretation

The current is very large because a substantial charge is transferred in a very short time.
