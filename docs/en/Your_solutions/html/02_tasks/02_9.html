Equation of Motion

Under linear drag, the total force is the sum of weight and air resistance:

\[m\frac{dv}{dt} = -mg - kv\]

As \(v\) increases in magnitude downward, drag increases until it balances gravity, reaching Terminal Velocity:

\[v_{term} = \frac{mg}{k}\]

Analytical Solutions

Velocity: \(v(t) = (v_0 + \frac{mg}{k})e^{-kt/m} - \frac{mg}{k}\)

Height: \(x(t) = 10 + \frac{m}{k}(v_0 + \frac{mg}{k})(1 - e^{-kt/m}) - \frac{mg}{k}t\)
