# Task 08.04 – Geostationary Orbit Altitude

## Problem Statement

Find the period and altitude required for a geostationary satellite.

## Theory

A geostationary satellite must match Earth's rotation and orbit in the equatorial plane. Its radius follows from circular orbital motion.

$$
T=24\text{ h}
$$

$$
r=\left(\frac{GMT^2}{4\pi^2}\right)^{1/3}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
T=86164\text{ s}
$$

$$
r=4.216\times10^7\text{ m}=42164\text{ km}
$$

$$
h=r-R_E=42164\text{ km}-6378\text{ km}=35786\text{ km}
$$

## Final Result

$$
h\approx35786\text{ km}
$$

## Interpretation

At this altitude the satellite completes one orbit per sidereal day and remains above nearly the same longitude.
