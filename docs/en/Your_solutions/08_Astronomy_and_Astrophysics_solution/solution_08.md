# Task 08.08 – Travel Time from Earth to Mars

## Problem Statement

For closest Earth-Mars distance $55$ million km, find travel time for light, a $40000\text{ km/h}$ spacecraft, and a $900\text{ km/h}$ airplane.

## Theory

For constant speed, time is distance divided by speed.

$$
t=\frac{d}{v}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
t_{light}=\frac{5.5\times10^7\text{ km}}{3.00\times10^5\text{ km/s}}=183\text{ s}=3.06\text{ min}
$$

$$
t_{ship}=\frac{5.5\times10^7}{40000}=1375\text{ h}=57.3\text{ days}
$$

$$
t_{plane}=\frac{5.5\times10^7}{900}=61111\text{ h}=6.98\text{ years}
$$

## Final Result

Light: $3.06$ minutes. Spacecraft: $57.3$ days. Airplane-speed travel: about $7.0$ years.

## Interpretation

Even the closest Mars distance is enormous compared with terrestrial travel distances.
