# Task 09.10 – Doppler Shift of Galaxy Light

## Problem Statement

A galaxy recedes at $5\times10^6\text{ m/s}$ and emits $486\text{ nm}$ light. Find observed wavelength using nonrelativistic and relativistic Doppler formulas.

## Theory

For small $v/c$, the nonrelativistic and relativistic Doppler shifts are close.

$$
\lambda_{obs}=\lambda_e\left(1+\frac{v}{c}\right)
$$

$$
\lambda_{obs}=\lambda_e\sqrt{\frac{1+v/c}{1-v/c}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\beta=\frac{5.0\times10^6}{3.00\times10^8}=0.01667
$$

$$
\lambda_{nr}=486(1+0.01667)=494.1\text{ nm}
$$

$$
\lambda_{rel}=486\sqrt{\frac{1.01667}{0.98333}}=494.2\text{ nm}
$$

## Final Result

The nonrelativistic result is $494.1\text{ nm}$ and the relativistic result is $494.2\text{ nm}$.

## Interpretation

The wavelength increases because the galaxy is receding, producing a redshift.
