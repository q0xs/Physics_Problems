# Task 11.02 – Minimum Voltage for X-Ray Production

## Problem Statement

Find the minimum potential difference needed to produce X-rays of wavelength $0.01\text{ nm}$.

## Theory

The maximum photon energy from an X-ray tube equals the electric energy gained by an electron.

$$
E=hf=\frac{hc}{\lambda}
$$

$$
eV=\frac{hc}{\lambda}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\lambda=0.01\text{ nm}=1.0\times10^{-11}\text{ m}
$$

$$
V=\frac{(6.626\times10^{-34})(3.00\times10^8)}{(1.602\times10^{-19})(1.0\times10^{-11})}=1.24\times10^5\text{ V}
$$

## Final Result

$$
V=1.24\times10^5\text{ V}=124\text{ kV}
$$

## Interpretation

Shorter wavelength X-rays require higher accelerating voltage because photon energy increases as wavelength decreases.
