# Task 09.01 – Chronology of the Early Universe

## Problem Statement

Place these events in chronological order: recombination, Big Bang nucleosynthesis, inflation, formation of first galaxies, and quark-gluon plasma era.

## Theory

Cosmic history is ordered by decreasing temperature and increasing time after the Big Bang.

$$
T\downarrow\quad\text{as}\quad t\uparrow
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Inflation occurred extremely early. The quark-gluon plasma era followed while the universe was still hot enough for free quarks and gluons. Big Bang nucleosynthesis formed light nuclei after the plasma cooled. Recombination formed neutral atoms much later. First galaxies formed after matter had time to gravitationally collapse.

## Final Result

Inflation, quark-gluon plasma era, Big Bang nucleosynthesis, recombination, formation of first galaxies.

## Interpretation

The order reflects cooling and structure formation: particles formed first, then nuclei, atoms, and finally galaxies.
