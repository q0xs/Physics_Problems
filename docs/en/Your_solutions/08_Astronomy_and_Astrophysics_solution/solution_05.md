# Task 08.05 – Escape Velocity from the Moon

## Problem Statement

Calculate escape velocity from the Moon and express it as a fraction of Earth's escape velocity $11.2\text{ km/s}$.

## Theory

Escape speed comes from equating kinetic energy to the gravitational binding energy per unit mass.

$$
v_e=\sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
v_e=\sqrt{\frac{2(6.674\times10^{-11})(7.35\times10^{22})}{1.737\times10^6}}
$$

$$
v_e=2.38\text{ km/s}
$$

$$
\frac{2.38}{11.2}=0.213
$$

## Final Result

The Moon's escape velocity is $2.38\text{ km/s}$, about $0.213$ of Earth's escape velocity.

## Interpretation

The Moon's weaker gravity and smaller radius make escape much easier than from Earth.
