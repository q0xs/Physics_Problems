# Task 10 – Speed of Light in Diamond

## Problem Statement

What is the speed of light in a diamond, which has an index of refraction $n = 2.42$?

## Theory

The speed of light in a medium is related to the refractive index by

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

where

- $v$ is the speed of light in the medium,
- $c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$ is the speed of light in vacuum,
- $n$ is the index of refraction.

A larger refractive index means that light travels more slowly in the medium.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Given:

$$
c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

$$
n = 2.42
$$

Use the relation

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8}{2.42}
$$

Evaluate the division:

$$
v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

$$
v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

Light travels much more slowly in diamond than in vacuum because diamond has a large refractive index. This strong reduction in speed is associated with the strong optical bending and high brilliance observed in diamonds.
