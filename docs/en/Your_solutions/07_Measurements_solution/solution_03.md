# Task 07.03 – Ohm's Law Resistance Uncertainty

## Problem Statement

Resistance is calculated from $R=V/I$. If $V=(10.0\pm0.2)\text{ V}$ and $I=(2.00\pm0.05)\text{ A}$, find $R$ and its uncertainty.

## Theory

For a quotient, independent relative uncertainties combine in quadrature.

$$
R=\frac{V}{I}
$$

$$
\frac{\Delta R}{R}=\sqrt{\left(\frac{\Delta V}{V}\right)^2+\left(\frac{\Delta I}{I}\right)^2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
R=\frac{10.0}{2.00}=5.00\,\Omega
$$

$$
\frac{\Delta R}{R}=\sqrt{(0.2/10.0)^2+(0.05/2.00)^2}=0.0320
$$

$$
\Delta R=(5.00)(0.0320)=0.16\,\Omega
$$

## Final Result

$$
R=(5.00\pm0.16)\,\Omega
$$

## Interpretation

The current measurement dominates slightly because its fractional uncertainty is larger.
