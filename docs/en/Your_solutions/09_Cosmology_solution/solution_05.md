# Task 09.05 – Length of a Galactic Year

## Problem Statement

The Solar System orbits the Milky Way center at radius $27000$ light-years and speed $220\text{ km/s}$. Estimate the orbital period.

## Theory

The galactic year is circumference divided by orbital speed.

$$
T=\frac{2\pi r}{v}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
r=27000(9.461\times10^{12})\text{ km}=2.55\times10^{17}\text{ km}
$$

$$
T=\frac{2\pi(2.55\times10^{17})}{220}=7.30\times10^{15}\text{ s}
$$

$$
T=2.31\times10^8\text{ yr}
$$

## Final Result

$$
T\approx2.3\times10^8\text{ yr}
$$

## Interpretation

A galactic orbit takes hundreds of millions of years because the Milky Way is extremely large.
