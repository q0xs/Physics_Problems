# Task 08.06 – Solar Surface Gravity

## Problem Statement

Calculate gravitational acceleration at the surface of the Sun and compare it with Earth gravity.

## Theory

Surface gravity follows Newton's law using the object's mass and radius.

$$
g=\frac{GM}{R^2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
g_S=\frac{(6.674\times10^{-11})(2.0\times10^{30})}{(6.96\times10^8)^2}=275\text{ m/s}^2
$$

$$
\frac{g_S}{g_E}=\frac{275}{9.81}=28.0
$$

## Final Result

Solar surface gravity is about $275\text{ m/s}^2$, or $28$ times Earth gravity.

## Interpretation

A person's weight would be much larger because the Sun's mass is enormous, despite its large radius.
