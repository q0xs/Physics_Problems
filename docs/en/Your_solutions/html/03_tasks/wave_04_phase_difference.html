Step-by-Step Physics

Formula

The phase difference between two points separated by $\Delta x$ is $\Delta\phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\Delta x$.

Normalized form

If the separation is written as a fraction of a wavelength, $\Delta x = r\lambda$, then $\Delta\phi = 2\pi r$.

Interpretation

One full wavelength corresponds to $2\pi$ radians or $360^\circ$. Therefore, a fraction of a wavelength produces the same fraction of a full phase cycle.

This task

For $\Delta x = \lambda/3$, the phase difference is $\Delta\phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}$, which is equal to $120^\circ$.
