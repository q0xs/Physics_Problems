# Task 11.03 – Photoelectric Effect for Potassium

## Problem Statement

Potassium has work function $2.3\text{ eV}$. Find the maximum kinetic energy of electrons ejected by light of wavelength $350\text{ nm}$.

## Theory

The photoelectric equation subtracts the work function from photon energy.

$$
K_{max}=\frac{hc}{\lambda}-\phi
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
E_\gamma=\frac{1240\text{ eV nm}}{350\text{ nm}}=3.54\text{ eV}
$$

$$
K_{max}=3.54-2.30=1.24\text{ eV}
$$

## Final Result

$$
K_{max}=1.24\text{ eV}
$$

## Interpretation

Emission occurs because the photon energy exceeds the work function.
