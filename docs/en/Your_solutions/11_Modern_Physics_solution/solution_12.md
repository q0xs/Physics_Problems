# Task 11.12 – Beta-Minus Decay of Carbon-14

## Problem Statement

Give a balanced beta-minus decay equation starting with $^{14}_{6}\text{C}$.

## Theory

In beta-minus decay, a neutron converts into a proton, emitting an electron and an antineutrino. The mass number is unchanged and atomic number increases by one.

$$
{}^{A}_{Z}X\rightarrow{}^{A}_{Z+1}Y+{}^{0}_{-1}e+\bar{\nu}_e
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
{}^{14}_{6}\text{C}\rightarrow{}^{14}_{7}\text{N}+{}^{0}_{-1}e+\bar{\nu}_e
$$

## Final Result

$$
{}^{14}_{6}\text{C}\rightarrow{}^{14}_{7}\text{N}+{}^{0}_{-1}e+\bar{\nu}_e
$$

## Interpretation

The antineutrino is required for conservation of energy, momentum, and lepton number.
