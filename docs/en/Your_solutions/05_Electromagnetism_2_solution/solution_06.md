# Task 05.06 – Electromagnetic Wave Parameters

## Problem Statement

An electromagnetic wave has electric field $E_y(x,t)=100\sin(10^7x-\omega t)\text{ V/m}$. Determine the propagation direction, wavelength, angular frequency, and magnetic-field equation.

## Theory

A wave of the form $\sin(kx-\omega t)$ travels in the positive $x$ direction. In vacuum, $\omega=ck$, $\lambda=2\pi/k$, and $B_0=E_0/c$.

$$
k = 10^7\text{ rad/m}
$$

$$
\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{k}
$$

$$
\omega = ck
$$

$$
B_0 = \frac{E_0}{c}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{10^7} = 6.28\times10^{-7}\text{ m}
$$

$$
\omega = (3.00\times10^8)(10^7) = 3.00\times10^{15}\text{ rad/s}
$$

$$
B_0 = \frac{100}{3.00\times10^8} = 3.33\times10^{-7}\text{ T}
$$

Since $\vec{E}$ is in the $y$ direction and the wave travels in $+x$, the magnetic field points in the $z$ direction.

## Final Result

$$
B_z(x,t)=3.33\times10^{-7}\sin(10^7x-3.00\times10^{15}t)\text{ T}
$$

## Interpretation

The electric and magnetic fields are perpendicular to each other and to the direction of wave propagation.
