# Task 05.03 – Biot-Savart Law for a Short Current Segment

## Problem Statement

A small segment of a line wire of length $0.1 \text{ m}$ carries a current of $3 \text{ A}$. The segment is located at a distance of $0.2 \text{ m}$ from a point $P$. Calculate the magnetic field at point $P$ due to this current segment, assuming the segment is perpendicular to the line connecting it to $P$.

## Theory

For a short straight current element, the Biot-Savart law gives the approximate field contribution. The angle is $90^\circ$, so $\sin\theta=1$.

$$
\Delta B = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{I\Delta l\sin\theta}{r^2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute $I=3\text{ A}$, $\Delta l=0.1\text{ m}$, $r=0.2\text{ m}$, and $\sin\theta=1$:

$$
\Delta B = 10^{-7}\frac{(3)(0.1)}{(0.2)^2}
$$

$$
\Delta B = 7.5 \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\Delta B = 7.5 \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}
$$

## Interpretation

The field is small because the current element is short and the magnetic field contribution decreases with the square of distance.
