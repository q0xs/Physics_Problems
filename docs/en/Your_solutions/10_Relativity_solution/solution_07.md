# Task 10.07 – Relativistic Velocity Transformation

## Problem Statement

A rocket moves at $0.8c$ away from Earth and shoots a probe forward at $0.5c$ relative to the rocket. Find the probe speed classically and relativistically.

## Theory

Classical velocity addition simply adds speeds, but special relativity uses a transformation that prevents speeds above $c$.

$$
u=\frac{u^\prime+v}{1+u^\prime v/c^2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
u_{classical}=0.5c+0.8c=1.3c
$$

$$
u_{rel}=\frac{0.5c+0.8c}{1+(0.5)(0.8)}=0.929c
$$

## Final Result

Classical result: $1.3c$; relativistic result: $0.929c$.

## Interpretation

The relativistic formula preserves the speed of light as an upper limit.
