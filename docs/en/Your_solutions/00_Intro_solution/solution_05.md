# Task 05 – Trigonometry

## Problem Statement

A vector $\vec{A}$ has magnitude $15$ and makes an angle of $\theta = 60^\circ$ with the horizontal axis.

Determine its horizontal and vertical components.

## Theory

A vector can be decomposed into perpendicular components along the horizontal and vertical axes.

If a vector has magnitude $A$ and makes an angle $\theta$ with the horizontal axis, then its components are

$$
A_x = A \cos\theta
$$

and

$$
A_y = A \sin\theta
$$

where:

$A_x$ is the horizontal component

$A_y$ is the vertical component

For the special angle $60^\circ$, the trigonometric values are

$$
\cos 60^\circ = \frac{1}{2}
$$

and

$$
\sin 60^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

The vector magnitude is

$$
A = 15
$$

and the angle is

$$
\theta = 60^\circ
$$

### Horizontal Component

Use the formula

$$
A_x = A \cos\theta
$$

Substitute the given values:

$$
A_x = 15 \cos 60^\circ
$$

Use the known value of $\cos 60^\circ$:

$$
A_x = 15 \cdot \frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

### Vertical Component

Use the formula

$$
A_y = A \sin\theta
$$

Substitute the given values:

$$
A_y = 15 \sin 60^\circ
$$

Use the known value of $\sin 60^\circ$:

$$
A_y = 15 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

To obtain a decimal approximation,

$$
A_y \approx 12.99
$$

## Final Result

The horizontal component is

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

The vertical component is

$$
A_y = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 12.99
$$

## Interpretation

The original vector is resolved into two perpendicular parts. The value $A_x = 7.5$ gives the projection of the vector on the horizontal axis, while $A_y \approx 12.99$ gives the projection on the vertical axis. Because the angle is $60^\circ$, the vertical component is larger than the horizontal component.
