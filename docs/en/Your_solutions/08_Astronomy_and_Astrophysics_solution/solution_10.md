# Task 08.10 – Geometric Height of the Atmosphere

## Problem Statement

A sunset delay of $40$ minutes corresponds to solar depression angle $\phi$. With $R_E=6370\text{ km}$ and $\cos\phi=R_E/(R_E+h)$, find $\phi$ and $h$.

## Theory

Earth rotates $360^\circ$ in $24$ hours, or $15^\circ$ per hour. The height follows from the grazing-ray geometry.

$$
\phi=15^\circ\text{/h}\times t
$$

$$
h=R_E\left(\frac{1}{\cos\phi}-1\right)
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
t=40\text{ min}=\frac{2}{3}\text{ h}
$$

$$
\phi=15^\circ\times\frac{2}{3}=10^\circ
$$

$$
h=6370\left(\frac{1}{\cos10^\circ}-1\right)=98.3\text{ km}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\phi=10^\circ
$$

$$
h=98.3\text{ km}
$$

## Interpretation

The sharp-edge atmosphere model is idealized, but it gives a height comparable to the upper atmosphere.
