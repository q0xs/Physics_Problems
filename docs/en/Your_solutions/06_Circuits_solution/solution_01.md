# Task 06.01 – Series and Parallel Resistor Networks

## Problem Statement

Three resistors $R_1=15\,\Omega$, $R_2=30\,\Omega$, and $R_3=50\,\Omega$ are connected to a $12\text{ V}$ battery. Find the equivalent resistance and battery current for series and parallel connections.

## Theory

Series resistances add directly. Parallel conductances add directly. Battery current follows from Ohm's law.

$$
R_s=R_1+R_2+R_3
$$

$$
\frac{1}{R_p}=\frac{1}{R_1}+\frac{1}{R_2}+\frac{1}{R_3}
$$

$$
I=\frac{V}{R}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
R_s=15+30+50=95\,\Omega
$$

$$
I_s=\frac{12}{95}=0.126\text{ A}
$$

$$
\frac{1}{R_p}=\frac{1}{15}+\frac{1}{30}+\frac{1}{50}=0.120
$$

$$
R_p=8.33\,\Omega
$$

$$
I_p=\frac{12}{8.33}=1.44\text{ A}
$$

## Final Result

$$
R_s=95\,\Omega,\quad I_s=0.126\text{ A}
$$

$$
R_p=8.33\,\Omega,\quad I_p=1.44\text{ A}
$$

## Interpretation

The parallel circuit draws more current because it provides multiple conducting paths and therefore has a lower equivalent resistance.
