# Task 10.04 – Energy to Accelerate a Proton

## Problem Statement

How much energy in GeV is required to accelerate a proton from rest to $0.99c$? The proton rest energy is $938\text{ MeV}$.

## Theory

The required kinetic energy is the relativistic kinetic energy, not the classical expression.

$$
K=(\gamma-1)mc^2
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\gamma=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-0.99^2}}=7.09
$$

$$
K=(7.09-1)(938\text{ MeV})=5710\text{ MeV}=5.71\text{ GeV}
$$

## Final Result

$$
K=5.71\text{ GeV}
$$

## Interpretation

Near light speed, energy rises steeply because the Lorentz factor increases rapidly.
