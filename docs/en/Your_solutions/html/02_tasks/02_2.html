Core Principles

A mass on a spring performs Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM) when the restoring force is proportional to the displacement. The position \(x(t)\) follows a sinusoidal function:

\[x(t) = A \cos(\omega t + \phi)\]

Spring Stiffness

The angular frequency is determined by the ratio of the spring stiffness to the mass:

\[\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \implies k = m\omega^2\]

Mechanical Energy

Total energy \(E\) is the sum of Kinetic and Potential energy. In an ideal system (no damping), it remains constant:

\[E_{total} = \frac{1}{2} k A^2 = \frac{1}{2} m \omega^2 A^2\]
Note: While position \(x\) varies with \(\cos\), velocity \(v\) varies with \(\sin\). The energy oscillates between spring potential (\(\frac{1}{2}kx^2\)) and kinetic (\(\frac{1}{2}mv^2\)).
