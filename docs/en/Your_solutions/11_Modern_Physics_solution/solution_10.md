# Task 11.10 – Radioactive Half-Life of Cobalt-60

## Problem Statement

Cobalt-60 has half-life $5.27$ years. If the sample begins at $100\text{ g}$, find the mass remaining after about $21$ years.

## Theory

Radioactive decay follows an exponential half-life law.

$$
N=N_0\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{t/T_{1/2}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
\frac{t}{T_{1/2}}=\frac{21}{5.27}=3.99
$$

$$
m=100\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{3.99}=6.32\text{ g}
$$

## Final Result

$$
m\approx6.3\text{ g}
$$

## Interpretation

About four half-lives have passed, so roughly one sixteenth of the original material remains.
