# Task 06.12 – Transformer Currents

## Problem Statement

A transformer has $N_p=1000$ primary turns and $N_s=200$ secondary turns. If $V_p=120\text{ V}$ AC and $I_s=3\text{ A}$, find $V_s$ and $I_p$.

## Theory

For an ideal transformer, voltage is proportional to turns and input power equals output power.

$$
\frac{V_s}{V_p}=\frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

$$
V_pI_p=V_sI_s
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
V_s=120\frac{200}{1000}=24\text{ V}
$$

$$
I_p=\frac{V_sI_s}{V_p}=\frac{(24)(3)}{120}=0.60\text{ A}
$$

## Final Result

$$
V_s=24\text{ V}
$$

$$
I_p=0.60\text{ A}
$$

## Interpretation

The transformer steps voltage down by a factor of five and steps current up by the same factor on the secondary side.
