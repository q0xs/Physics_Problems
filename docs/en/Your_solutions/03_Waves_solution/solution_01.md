# Task 01 – Wave Properties

## Problem Statement

A sound wave in air has a frequency of 440 Hz. If the speed of sound in air is 343 m/s, determine its wavelength. Then determine its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is 1482 m/s.

## Theory

The relationship between wave speed, frequency, and wavelength is

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

Solving for wavelength:

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Wavelength in Air

Given:

- $v_{\text{air}} = 343 \, \text{m/s}$
- $f = 440 \, \text{Hz}$

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{343}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.78 \, \text{m}
$$

### Wavelength in Water

Given:

- $v_{\text{water}} = 1482 \, \text{m/s}$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{1482}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \, \text{m}
$$

## Final Result

- $\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.78 \, \text{m}$
- $\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \, \text{m}$

## Interpretation

The wavelength increases in water because the wave speed is higher, while the frequency remains constant.
