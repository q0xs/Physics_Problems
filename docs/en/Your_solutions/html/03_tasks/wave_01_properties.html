Physical insight
A higher wave speed produces a longer wavelength when frequency is fixed.

Step-by-Step Physics

Fundamental relation

The wave equation connecting speed, frequency, and wavelength is $v = f\lambda$.

Solving for wavelength gives $\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$.

Why the wavelength changes

The source fixes the frequency. When the sound enters another medium, the wave speed changes because the medium changes. Since $f$ remains the same, the wavelength must adjust.

Interpretation

If the medium transmits sound faster, the crest-to-crest spacing becomes larger. That is why sound in water has a much longer wavelength than in air at the same frequency.
