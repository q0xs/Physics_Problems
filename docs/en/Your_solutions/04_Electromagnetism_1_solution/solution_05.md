# Task 05 – Electric Field Required for Proton Levitation

## Problem Statement

Determine the electric field strength required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity at the Earth's surface.

Given:

$$
m_p = 1.67 \times 10^{-27}\,\mathrm{kg}
$$

$$
e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\,\mathrm{C}
$$

$$
g = 9.8\,\mathrm{m/s^2}
$$

## Theory

A proton levitates when the upward electric force balances the downward gravitational force.

The gravitational force is

$$
F_g = m_p g
$$

The electric force on a charge in an electric field is

$$
F_e = qE
$$

At equilibrium,

$$
F_e = F_g
$$

Therefore,

$$
qE = m_p g
$$

and the required electric field is

$$
E = \frac{m_p g}{q}
$$

Since the proton is positively charged, the electric force points in the same direction as the field.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Gravitational force on the proton

Substitute the given mass and gravitational acceleration:

$$
F_g = m_p g
$$

$$
F_g = (1.67 \times 10^{-27})(9.8)
$$

$$
F_g = 1.6366 \times 10^{-26}\,\mathrm{N}
$$

### Required electric field

Since the proton charge is

$$
q = e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\,\mathrm{C}
$$

the field required for levitation is

$$
E = \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7}\,\mathrm{N/C}
$$

Because

$$
1\,\mathrm{N/C} = 1\,\mathrm{V/m}
$$

the same result may be written as

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7}\,\mathrm{V/m}
$$

### Direction of the field

The weight acts downward, so the electric force must act upward to balance it.

A proton has positive charge, so the electric field must point upward.

## Final Result

The electric field required to levitate a proton is

$$
E \approx 1.0 \times 10^{-7}\,\mathrm{N/C}
$$

directed upward.

## Interpretation

The required field is extremely small because the proton mass is extremely small. This illustrates that for microscopic charged particles, electric forces can easily compete with and exceed gravitational forces.
