# Task 02 – String Harmonics

## Problem Statement

A guitar string is 64 cm long and has a fundamental frequency of 330 Hz. Determine the wave speed.

## Theory

For the fundamental mode of a string fixed at both ends:

$$
\lambda_1 = 2L
$$

Wave speed:

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Convert length:

$$
L = 0.64 \, \text{m}
$$

Wavelength:

$$
\lambda = 2L = 2 \times 0.64 = 1.28 \, \text{m}
$$

Wave speed:

$$
v = f \lambda = 330 \times 1.28
$$

$$
v = 422.4 \, \text{m/s}
$$

## Final Result

$$
v = 422.4 \, \text{m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

The wave speed depends on the tension and mass density of the string, not on frequency alone.
