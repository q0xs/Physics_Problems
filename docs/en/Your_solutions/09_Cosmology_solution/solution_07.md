# Task 09.07 – Communication Across the Milky Way

## Problem Statement

Estimate the time to exchange greetings between civilizations on opposite ends of the Milky Way if signals travel at light speed.

## Theory

Light takes one year to travel one light-year. A mutual exchange requires a round trip.

$$
t_{\text{round}}=2\frac{D}{c}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Using a Milky Way diameter of about $100000\text{ ly}$,

$$
t_{\text{one-way}}=100000\text{ yr}
$$

$$
t_{\text{round}}=200000\text{ yr}
$$

## Final Result

One greeting takes about $100000$ years to arrive; a reply returns after about $200000$ years total.

## Interpretation

Even light-speed communication is slow on galactic length scales.
