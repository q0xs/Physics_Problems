Task 05 — Echo Ranging

Use the round-trip travel time of sound to determine the distance to a reflecting object.

Interactive Controls

Computed Results

Round-trip distance
343.0000 m
Distance to cliff
171.5000 m
Reason for division by 2
The measured time includes the journey to the cliff and the return journey to the listener.

Step-by-Step Physics

Travel geometry

The echo time measures the total round trip of the sound pulse.

Distance formula

The one-way distance is $d = \frac{vt}{2}$.

Interpretation

Echo ranging is one of the simplest examples of time-of-flight measurement and is the same basic principle used in sonar and other ranging systems.
