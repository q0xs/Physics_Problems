# Task 06.11 – Power and Energy in a Resistor

## Problem Statement

Find the power dissipated by a $100\,\Omega$ resistor connected to $50\text{ V}$ and the energy consumed in $5\text{ min}$.

## Theory

For a resistor with known voltage, power is $V^2/R$. Energy is power multiplied by time.

$$
P=\frac{V^2}{R}
$$

$$
E=Pt
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
P=\frac{50^2}{100}=25\text{ W}
$$

$$
t=5\text{ min}=300\text{ s}
$$

$$
E=(25)(300)=7500\text{ J}
$$

## Final Result

$$
P=25\text{ W}
$$

$$
E=7.50\times10^3\text{ J}
$$

## Interpretation

The resistor converts electrical energy into thermal energy at a constant rate of $25\text{ J/s}$.
