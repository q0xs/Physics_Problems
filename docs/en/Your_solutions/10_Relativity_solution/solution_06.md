# Task 10.06 – Mass-Energy Released by an Atomic Bomb

## Problem Statement

The Little Boy bomb converted about $0.7\text{ g}$ of mass into energy. Find the energy and kiloton equivalent using $1\text{ kt}=4.184\times10^{12}\text{ J}$.

## Theory

Einstein's mass-energy relation converts lost mass into released energy.

$$
E=mc^2
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
m=0.7\text{ g}=7.0\times10^{-4}\text{ kg}
$$

$$
E=(7.0\times10^{-4})(3.00\times10^8)^2=6.3\times10^{13}\text{ J}
$$

$$
\frac{6.3\times10^{13}}{4.184\times10^{12}}=15.1\text{ kt}
$$

## Final Result

The energy is $6.3\times10^{13}\text{ J}$, about $15.1$ kilotons of TNT.

## Interpretation

A tiny mass defect corresponds to an enormous energy release because $c^2$ is very large.
